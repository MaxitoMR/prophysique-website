The 4 Metabolic Types

WHICH TYPE ARE YOU?

Your dominant gland determines how you store fat, respond to food, and what training actually works for your body. Take the 2-minute quiz to find yours.

A
The High-Stress Achiever

Adrenal

Stress-driven. Stores fat in the midsection. Craves salty & fatty foods. Common in high-performing professionals.

  • Belly fat accumulation
  • Cortisol-dominant metabolism
  • Gains weight under stress
  • Needs calming nutrition strategy
T
The Energy Metabolism Type

Thyroid

Metabolism-driven. Weight fluctuates. Craves starches & sweets. Swings from sluggish to energetic.

  • Upper body weight gain
  • Energy peaks and crashes
  • Sensitive to carb timing
  • Needs metabolic support
G
The Hormone-Sensitive Type

Gonadal

Hormone-driven. Stores fat in hips & thighs. Craves rich & spicy foods. Sensitive to hormonal cycles.

  • Lower body / hip-dominant storage
  • Hormonal cycle sensitivity
  • Responds well to resistance training
  • Needs hormonal balance focus
P
The Nurturer Type

Pituitary

Growth-driven. Stores fat evenly all over. Craves dairy & creamy foods. Responsive to stress and sleep.

  • Even fat distribution
  • Responsive to stress & sleep
  • Responds to training intensity
  • Needs growth hormone support

Nutrition By Type

EAT FOR YOUR TYPE.

A

Adrenal

High protein. Moderate fat. Limit sodium. Avoid stimulants. Focus on calming the stress response.

T

Thyroid

Balanced macros. Limit refined carbs & sugar. Iodine-rich foods. Support metabolic function.

G

Gonadal

Lower fat. Moderate carbs. Cruciferous veggies to balance estrogen and support lower body leanness.

P

Pituitary

Limit dairy. Higher vegetables & lean protein. Support growth hormone with quality sleep nutrition.

Training By Type

TRAIN FOR YOUR TYPE.

A

Adrenal

Low-intensity steady cardio + strength. Avoid overtraining. Recovery is your secret weapon.

T

Thyroid

HIIT + full-body circuits. Rev the metabolism. Frequent movement throughout the day.

G

Gonadal

Lower-body focused strength + cardio. Resistance training balances hormones naturally.

P

Pituitary

Full-body compound lifts. Sprint intervals. Short intense sessions to stimulate growth hormone.

Common Mistake

FIGHTING YOUR GLANDS IS WHY YOU'RE STUCK.

Most people train against their dominant gland. An Adrenal type doing brutal HIIT daily raises cortisol and gains fat. A Thyroid type doing slow cardio stalls their metabolism. Working with your biology changes everything.
